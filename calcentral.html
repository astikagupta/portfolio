CALCENTRAL BUDGET TOOL

A web based budget tool that empowers
students to better understand their finances
and plan for the future.

WHAT: Group Course Project

WHERE: Master Of Information Management
& Systems, UC Berkeley

WHEN: August 2015 - December 2015

SKILLS: User Research, User Experience
Design, Paper, Lo-Fi and Interactive Prototyping,
Contextual Inquiries, Personas, Storyboarding

SOFTWARE: Photoshop, Balsamiq,
Justinmind,Omnigraffle

Problem

The existing campus-wide webiste called CalCentral currently is not equipped with a budgetting tool that can help students to keep a track of their monthly finances and plan their future finances better. We identified this gap and designed a tool for budgetting that can be integrated with the existing website. It also allows them to make informed financial decisions by giving them an opportunity to test out potential future financial scenarios and see how those affect their bottom line.

Solution

The main goal of the tool is to let the students track their monthly expensis, save for future investments and plan their finances better. We formed two focus groups for grad and undergrad students respectively and conducted contextual inquiries to gauge the requirement of a budget tool on the webiste. We then engaged in brainstorming, affinity diagramming and heurestic evaluations to come up with paper, lo-fi and interactive prototypes and conducted user testing to test them out.

Reflection

This project was a very fruitful experience for me as I was involved in every stage of the process. The lessons I learnt during the project were the different uses and limitations of each prototyping tool that I used and how to use them wisely, importance of referring to user data and user engagement in the design process to ensure their needs are met and how the iterative process of prototyping allowed for constant user feedback and support to design the final tool.

Primary Personas

After performing contextual inqueries and in-depth interviews, we came up with 4 primary personas namely Angela,Julio, Sarah and Jason. They embodied the different backgrounds, traits and personalities of the students we interviewed. While preparing these personas, we tried to incorporate as many traits and personalities as we could so that we cater to the needs of students with diverse backgrounds and lifestyles.

Previous Next



Lo-fi Prototyping

After making the paper prototypes, we started with the lo-fi prototyping phase. We began with Balsamiq as that seemed to be very simple to use. Then we progressed to Justinmind and created the mock-ups for the entire tool. Finally, we chose Omnigraffle and the existing Calcentral screens to create the screens that would look exactly like those in the final tool after they are coded.
Using, various prototyping tools gave me a taste of the pros and cons of each of them. It gave me good practice of prototyping in different softwares and in this process I developed a strong penchant towards prototyping as a skill.
